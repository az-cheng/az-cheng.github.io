Aircraft Maneuver Recognition on Flight Test Data of C919

Background
During the summer of 2021, I interned at COMAC (Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China) flight test center in Shanghai. The company is the largest airplane manufacturer in China. It was a great opportunity to engage in the industry, make use of my skills and take them to the next level.

I conducted the research of “Aircraft Maneuver Recognition.” The maneuvers include circling, climb turning, rolling, as well as stalling. We used a variety of machine learning tools that identify specific data patterns correspond with such maneuvers. Here is a cool video showing the 90 degree rolling maneuver during test flight:


Model training
Trained on massive data with over hundreds of features, we were able to eventually build a model with very high precision. It was a breakthrough in the field of flight testing. Our model saved a huge amount of time for the test engineers by automating the important step of maneuver recognition, which further enhanced safety of flight testing.

During the process, I worked alongside experienced researchers and learned about using dimensionality reduction and feature engineering to process the data. Then, I independently applied support vector machine (SVM) models to the data. Here is a table combining the final results of precisions corresponding with the different algorithms.

Takeaways
In addition to the analysis, I saw the effort taken to manage a project like this on a large scale: how to communicate goals and expected outcomes from engineers to researchers, how to deliver data in secure ways, how to put all the different components together while keeping up with the timeline, and how to effectively present the results. I put up proposals of improving the infrastructure of the company using network optimizations and eventually some of my suggestions were adopted by the department. I am always looking to make a positive impact for the environment where I am in and I found it so rewarding.
